THE BRAHMAPUTRA



The Brahmaputra river originates from a glacial part of Kailash Peak of the Himalayas, then flows to the east over Tibet and finally

passes through Assam to the west Thereafter, The Brahmaputra rivers enters into Bangladesh nea Kurigram. Thereafter, This river

takes a turn towards south-east near Dewanganj and then flowing through the district of Mymensingh falls at Meghna to the south of

Bhairab Bazar. Dharla and Tista are the main tributaries and Bangshi and Sitalakhya are the major distributaries of the Brahmaputra

river.
