THE PADMA







The padma is one of the largest river of Bangladesh. This has originated from the Gangatri, glacier. Then it flows to the south-west

and then to the south-east taking the name of the Gangaes at Haridwar and enters into the plain lands. From this point the Gangas flow

through uttar Pradesh and Bihar State leaving dis tributary named Bhagirathi(Hooghly) river at Dhulian of Smurshidabad district in

West Bengal. The Bhagirathi falls into the Bay of Bengal.The main flow of the Ganges takes the name of padma at the south-western

point at Rajshahi district and flowing for about 145 kilometers through West Bengal and along the border of Bangladesh enters into

Bangladesh through the north-western point of Kustia district. Then it meets the jamuna riper at Daulatdia ghat. This combined stream

flows towards the south-east as the padma and meets the meghna. At chandpur. Afterwards, the combined flow of these three rivers

fall into the Bay of Bengal as the Meghna. The padma drains an area of 34,188 square kilometers. The Kumar, Mathabhanga, Bhairab,

Mahananda is the main tributary. Punarbhava, Pagla, Kulik and Tangan are the tributaries of Mahananda.
