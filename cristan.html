CHRISTIANITY





Main article: Christianity in Bangladesh Holy Rosary's Church in Dhaka.



Christianity arrived in what is now Bangladesh during the late sixteenth to early seventeenth centuries AD, through the Portuguese

traders and missionaries. Christians account for approximately 0.3% of the total population. Roman Catholicism is dominant; the

remaining Christians are predominantly Protestant
