BARISAL







Sunrise at Kuakata sea beach,Barisal Division is located in south-central Bangladesh, with an area of 13,644.85 km2,and with a

population of 8,147,000 at the 2011 Census (preliminary returns). It is bounded by Dhaka division on the north, the Bay of Bengal on

the south, Chittagong division on the east and Khulna division on the west. The administrative capital, Barisal city, lies in the Ganges

(Padma) River delta on an offshoot of the Arial Khan River (Kirtonkhola). Barisal division is criss-crossed by plenty of rivers that

earned her the nickname 'Dhan-Nodi-Kaal, Ei tine Barisal' (rice, river and canal built Barisal).

The Greater Barisal region (Barisal District along with five other neighbouring districts) was created Barisal Divisionon 1 January

1993

The Division is subdivided into six districts (zilas) and thence into 39 sub-districts (upazilas). Lower level administrative areas are

353 union parishads, 3159 mouzas, 12 municipalities, 25 wards and 4163 villages.

Kuakata sea beach is the main tourist spot in the division. It is the one of the two sea beaches in South Asia where both sunrise and

sunset at sea can be seen.


The religions in Barisal Division is Muslim, Hindu, Christianity and Buddhism
