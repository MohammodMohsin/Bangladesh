CHITTAGONG








Chittagong Division is geographically the largest of the seven administrative divisions of Bangladesh. It covers the most south-eastern

areas of the country, with a total area of 33,771.18 km^2 and a population at the 2011 Census (preliminary result) of 28,079,000.Ships

moored off Chittagong in the late 1820s.The Division is subdivided into eleven districts and thence into 99 sub-districts (upazilas).

The first six districts listed below comprise the north-western portion (37.6%) of the Division,while the remaining five comprise the

south-eastern portion (62.4%), the twoportions being separated by the lower (or Bangladeshi) stretch of the Feni River; the upland

districts of Khagrachhari, Rangamati and Bandarban together comprise that area previously known as the Chittagong Hill.
